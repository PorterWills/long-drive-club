6. DRIVING ON OPEN ROADS

6.1 Nature of Convoy Drives

Convoy Drives take place on public roads which are open to other road users. LDC provides route planning, convoy coordination, and the services of a Convoy Leader. LDC does not provide driving instruction, racing, timed driving, or competitive driving of any kind. The Convoy Drive is a social and leisure experience only.

You acknowledge that driving on public roads involves inherent risks which cannot be entirely eliminated, and that you participate in any Convoy Drive entirely at your own risk (subject to clause 8, Limitation of Liability).

For Convoy Drives, LDC may require you to provide separate online or event-day confirmation that you have read and understood the driving, insurance, safety, risk, and participant declaration requirements. LDC may refuse participation if that confirmation is not provided.

6.2 Eligibility and Licence Requirements

To participate in a Convoy Drive, you must:

  • be at least 25 years of age, unless LDC states a higher minimum age for a particular Convoy Drive;
  • hold a full, valid, and current driving licence issued in the United Kingdom (or an equivalent licence recognised for driving in England and Wales) appropriate to the class of Vehicle being driven;
  • produce your driving licence for inspection by LDC staff before the start of the Convoy Drive;
  • not have any driving ban, disqualification, or pending prosecution that would make it unlawful for you to drive;
  • not have more than 6 penalty points on your licence, unless LDC has expressly agreed otherwise in writing after reviewing the relevant circumstances;

You must have held a full driving licence for at least 2 years unless LDC states otherwise in writing. LDC may refuse participation if you cannot produce satisfactory evidence of your licence, driving history, identity, or eligibility, or if LDC reasonably considers that your participation would create an unacceptable safety, insurance, or legal risk.

6.3 Vehicle Requirements

Unless LDC expressly provides a Vehicle for your use, you must drive your own Vehicle on a Convoy Drive. Your Vehicle must:

  • be roadworthy and in a safe mechanical condition;
  • hold a valid MOT certificate (where applicable);
  • be taxed for road use;
  • be covered by a valid motor insurance policy providing at minimum the cover required by law (and you must ensure that participating in a convoy driving experience does not invalidate your insurance);
  • not be subject to any SORN (Statutory Off Road Notification).

LDC reserves the right to inspect any Vehicle before a Convoy Drive and to refuse participation to any Vehicle that, in its reasonable opinion, is not roadworthy or does not meet the requirements of this clause.

Unless LDC states otherwise, Vehicles must be road-legal private cars suitable for ordinary public-road use. LDC may refuse any Vehicle that is excessively noisy, visibly unsafe, leaking fluids, carrying unsafe modifications, using unsuitable tyres, lacking number plates or lights required by law, or otherwise unsuitable for the Route, weather, or Activity. You are responsible for ensuring that your Vehicle has sufficient fuel or charge and suitable breakdown cover for the whole Route.

6.4 Insurance

Subject always to LDC’s non-excludable legal duties, it is your responsibility to ensure that your Vehicle is insured for participation in a Convoy Drive. You must check with your insurer that your policy covers participation in an organised convoy driving experience on public roads and that the activity does not invalidate or restrict your cover. LDC does not provide motor insurance cover for Participants' Vehicles and accepts no liability for any loss, damage, or claims arising from your inadequate, invalid, or restricted insurance, except to the extent caused by LDC’s negligence or other breach of legal duty.

LDC may refuse participation if satisfactory evidence is not provided.

6.5 Health and Fitness

You must be in a fit and proper condition to drive safely. You must not participate in a Convoy Drive if:

  • you are under the influence of alcohol, drugs (whether illegal or prescription/over-the-counter medication that may impair driving), or any other substance that may affect your ability to drive safely;
  • you are suffering from any medical condition, illness, injury, or fatigue that may impair your ability to drive safely;

6.6 Safety Rules and Conduct

During any Convoy Drive, you must:

  • comply at all times with the Highway Code, all road traffic laws, speed limits, and any other legal requirements applicable to driving on public roads in England and Wales;
  • follow all reasonable instructions and directions given by the Convoy Leader or LDC staff;
  • drive at a speed and in a manner appropriate to the road conditions, traffic, and your own ability, regardless of the speed of other Participants;
  • maintain a safe following distance from other Vehicles in the convoy;
  • not use a handheld mobile phone or other handheld device while driving;
  • wear a seatbelt at all times (and ensure all passengers do likewise);
  • stop immediately if you consider it unsafe to continue, regardless of what other Participants are doing;
  • pull over and stop if directed to do so by the Convoy Leader, LDC staff, or any emergency service or police officer;
  • not overtake dangerously, drive on the wrong side of the road, jump red lights, or commit any driving offence;
  • not engage in racing, competitive driving, timed driving, or any attempt to keep up with or follow the convoy where doing so would require breaking the law or driving beyond your ability.
  • You must attend and comply with any safety briefing, route briefing, convoy instructions, radio or communication protocol, and written route notes provided by LDC. Those briefings, instructions, protocols, and route notes form part of these Terms for the relevant Convoy Drive.
  • LDC may alter, shorten, pause, split, reroute, postpone, or abandon a Convoy Drive at any time where LDC reasonably considers it appropriate for safety, legal, traffic, weather, operational, welfare, police, emergency-service, road-closure, or vehicle-breakdown reasons.
  • No route plan, convoy instruction, radio message, signal, conduct of the Convoy Leader, or action of another Participant is an instruction, permission, or encouragement to exceed speed limits, contravene traffic signs, drive dangerously, use a handheld device while driving, or ignore the Highway Code or any road traffic law.
